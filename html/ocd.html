Understanding OCD

Obsessive-Compulsive Disorder (OCD) is a mental health condition characterized by two main components: obsessions and compulsions.

Obsessions are intrusive, unwanted thoughts, images, or urges that cause significant anxiety or distress. Compulsions are repetitive behaviors or mental acts that a person feels driven to perform in response to these obsessions.

This page will help you understand the nature of OCD, its symptoms, and evidence-based strategies for managing it. Remember, recovery is possible with proper support and treatment.
