Dissociation

Understanding Dissociation

Dissociation is a psychological response where a person feels disconnected from their body, thoughts, feelings, or surroundings.

It can manifest as feeling like you're observing yourself from outside your body, memory gaps, or numbness to emotions. Dissociation often occurs as a coping mechanism during stress or trauma.

Grounding techniques and professional support can help you reconnect with the present moment and manage dissociative experiences.
